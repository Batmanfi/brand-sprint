## SECTION 7 — FAQs

### Before you book

**Q: How is a full rebrand and landing page possible in 10 days?**
A: Standard agencies run strategy, identity, and web sequentially with handoffs between teams. We run every layer in parallel with one senior designer across all of them. The work-hours are the same as a typical month-long engagement. The calendar time is compressed because there are no handoffs.

**Q: Do I need to write the page copy?**
A: No. We write every line of the landing page from the brand brief and your offer. You approve the copy at the same checkpoint where you approve the visual direction.

**Q: What if I have no brand assets to start from?**
A: Starting from scratch is what the sprint is built for. The kickoff call covers your positioning, your customer, and your direction. Bring answers to our discovery questions. We build the visual identity from there.

**Q: How much of my time will this actually take?**
A: Three 30-minute checkpoint reviews. Day 3 for brand direction. Day 6 for full identity. Day 10 for final files. Outside those, you do not need to be in the project.

**Q: What if I don't like the first direction?**
A: Unlimited revisions within scope. We revise until the direction is right. The 75% refund after week 1 covers the case where the fit isn't there at all.

**Q: Is this a subscription?**
A: No. $7,500 flat, one payment, one engagement. Designjoy's monthly subscription is a separate product. The sprint carries no ongoing obligation.

**Q: Is this right for my business?**
A: Yes if: you're a startup launching to market, or a company that has outgrown its current brand and needs a rebrand built and shipped together with the page. Not right if: you only need ad creatives, complex motion design, or video production. Those are out of scope.

**Q: 6-month agency timelines exist for a reason. What's actually different here?**
A: The 6-month timeline isn't from the work itself. It's from the structure — multiple teams, sequential handoffs, committee approvals, and scheduling lag between every phase. One senior designer running every layer in parallel removes every one of those bottlenecks. Same work, no waiting.
